🍅 Tomato Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Compost-rich, well-draining soil, 5 gal pot or in-ground, Support (cage/stake), Consistent deep watering, Calcium-rich fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times in the U.S.:
Southern: Mar–Apr & Aug–Sep
Western: Apr–May
Midwest: Late Apr–May
Northeast: May–Jun
Pacific NW: May–Jun

🌱 How to Grow: Bury 2/3 of stem, water 1–2× wk deeply, feed every 2–3 wk, prune suckers if desired, support early.

🍅 When to Harvest: Fully colored, slightly soft; pick often to maximize yield.

🌟 Quick Tips: Start seeds 6–8 wk before frost; mulch to conserve moisture and regulate soil temp.

🌶️ Jalapeño Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Rich soil, 3–5 gal pot, Consistent moisture, Optional stake, Balanced fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times:
Southern: Mar–Apr & Aug–Sep
Western: Apr–May
Midwest: Late Apr–May
Northeast: May–Jun
Pacific NW: May–Jun

🌱 How to Grow: Start indoors 8–10 wk before frost, plant deep, water evenly, feed 2–3 wk, support vines, watch pests.

🌶️ When to Harvest: Green: firm, 3–4″; Red: sweeter and spicier.

🌟 Quick Tips: Mulch saves moisture; plant after soil temps reach 60 °F; frequent picking boosts yield.

🥕 Carrot Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Deep sandy soil, 12″ container/bed, Even moisture during germination, Low-nitrogen fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times:
Southern: Sep–Mar
Western: Mar–Jun & Jul–Aug
Midwest: Apr–Jun & Jul–Aug
Northeast: Apr–Jun & Jul–Aug
Pacific NW: Apr–Jun & Jul–Aug

🌱 How to Grow: Direct sow, ¼″ deep; keep soil damp; thin to 2–3″; feed lightly.

🥕 When to Harvest: 60–80 days; harvest when desired size; freeze-free ground OK.

🌟 Quick Tips: Mulch cools soil; succession plant every 2–3 wk; loose soil = straight roots.
×

🌽 Sweet Corn Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Compost-rich soil, Plenty of space (4×4’), Consistent watering, High-nitrogen fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times:
Southern: Mar–May & Jul–Aug
Western/Midwest: Apr–Jun
Northeast/Pacific NW: May–Jun

🌱 How to Grow: Direct sow in blocks; 1–2″ deep; water evenly; feed at seed and 12–18″; mulch; weed carefully.

🌽 When to Harvest: 60–100 days; look for brown silks, full husks, milky kernels.

🌟 Quick Tips: Succession sow every 2–3 wk; net seedlings from birds; “Three Sisters” combo works great.

🌿 Herb Growing Guide: Basil, Parsley, Chives & Cilantro

✅ What You Need: 6+ hrs Sun, Well-draining soil, 6″ pots or garden bed, Regular moisture, Light fertilizer 4–6 wk.

📅 Best Planting Times:
Southern: Mar–Sep
Western: Apr–Aug
Midwest/Northeast/Pacific NW: May–Aug

🌱 How to Grow: Sow or transplant; water evenly; pinch often for bushy growth.

🍴 When to Harvest: Cut outer leaves from 6″ height; harvest morning for best flavor.

🌟 Quick Tips: Basil loves heat; parsley tolerates shade; chives regrow annually; cilantro bolts—succession sow.

🍉 Watermelon Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Loose compost soil, Tons of space, Deep watering, Start with nitrogen, switch to bloom fertilizer.

📅 Best Planting Times:
Southern: Mar–Jun
Western/Midwest/Northeast/Pacific NW: Apr–Jun

🌱 How to Grow: Direct sow, 1″ deep, 2–3′ apart; water deeply on fruit; mulch.

🍉 When to Harvest: 70–100 days; creamy ground spot, dried tendril, hollow sound.

🌟 Quick Tips: Reduce water 1 wk before harvest for sweetness; bush varieties fit small gardens.

🎃 Pumpkin Growing Guide

✅ What You Need: Full Sun, Rich compost soil, Wide space, Deep watering, Nitrogen early, bloom fertilizer later.

📅 Best Planting Times:
Southern: Mar–Jul
Western/Midwest/Northeast/Pacific NW: Apr–Jun

🌸 Calandrinia Growing Guide

✅ What You Need:

  • 🌞 Full Sun to Partial Shade
  • 🌱 Well-draining succulent soil mix
  • 🪴 Container or garden space
  • 💧 Water source: Light, infrequent watering

📅 Best Planting Times:

  • Spring and Summer are ideal
  • Can be planted year-round in warm climates

🌿 How to Grow:

  1. Take healthy stem cuttings, let them callous for 1-2 days.
  2. Plant directly into well-draining soil.
  3. Water lightly after planting, then weekly until established.
  4. Once mature, reduce watering significantly — it can survive drought.
  5. Can self-seed if allowed to flower and drop seeds naturally.

💧 When to Water:

  • Spring/Summer: Weekly until established, then only when soil is fully dry.
  • Fall/Winter: Minimal water, once every 3-4 weeks.

🌟 Quick Tips:

  • Great drought-tolerant option for hot, sunny spots.
  • Easy to propagate from cuttings or self-seeding.
  • Bright purple flowers attract pollinators.

🌵 Spiny Texas Prickly Pear Growing Guide

✅ What You Need:

  • 🌞 Full Sun
  • 🌱 Gritty, well-draining soil
  • 🪴 Outdoor garden or large container
  • 💧 Water source: Rare watering

📅 Best Planting Times:

  • Spring or Early Summer

🌿 How to Grow:

  1. Cut a healthy pad in spring and let it cure for 3-5 days.
  2. Plant pad upright, burying the base about 1-2 inches deep.
  3. Water lightly after planting, then wait until soil is fully dry to water again.
  4. Minimal maintenance — thrives in neglect!

💧 When to Water:

  • Water once every 3-4 weeks in warm months.
  • Withhold water in winter — they can go dormant.

🌟 Quick Tips:

  • Wear gloves! Spines can be sharp and irritating.
  • Very low-maintenance and excellent for drought-tolerant gardens.
  • Can spread into a gorgeous, sprawling patch over time.

🌺 Plumeria Growing Guide

✅ What You Need:

  • 🌞 Full Sun (6-8 hours minimum)
  • 🌱 Well-draining soil or cactus mix
  • 🪴 Large container or ground planting in warm climates
  • 💧 Water deeply but infrequently

📅 Best Planting Times:

  • Spring and Early Summer are best

🌿 How to Grow:

  1. Use fresh, calloused plumeria cuttings.
  2. Plant in well-draining soil, water sparingly at first.
  3. Provide bright sun and minimal water until roots form.
  4. Once rooted, water every 7-10 days when soil is dry.

💧 When to Water:

  • Spring/Summer: Water deeply when soil is fully dry.
  • Fall/Winter: Water rarely — plumeria can go dormant.

🌟 Quick Tips:

  • Fragrant, tropical flowers bloom in summer.
  • Bring indoors or protect from frost in winter.
  • Easy to propagate — shipped with rooting hormone helps success.
